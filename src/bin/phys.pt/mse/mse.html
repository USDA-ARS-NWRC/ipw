mse

IPW User Command
Category - Environmental Physics and Properties: Point

NAME

mse - moist static energy (MSE)

SYNOPSIS

mse [ - i ]

DESCRIPTION

mse is one of a group of commands designed to simulate energy, climate, or hydrologic processes or properties at a point. Most were originally written as "qdips" (Quick and Dirty Image Processing System) programs by D. Marks, during the 1980's at UCSB.

mse calculates moist static energy MSE (J/kg) from air temp Ta, vapor pressure ea, for specified elevation level z, from input of Ta (C), ea (Pa), and z (m). Or inverts moist static energy equation to calculate vapor pressure for specified elevation level, from input of Ta (C), MSE (J/kg), and z (m).

(see Dynamic Meteorology, Holton, 1979)

Input is to stdin; output to stdout.

OPTIONS

-i
Calculate vapor pressure from air temp, elev, and moist static energy

OPERANDS

none

EXAMPLES

	paste ta z vp pa | mse
